What is the food chart

The foodchart are the guidelines from the Australian Department of Health and Aging. These guidelines have changed alot over the years, with many poritions on the chart either getting larger or smaller sections of the pie. Look at the chart below. Identify what you should eat plenty of and what you shouldn't eat to much of.

As you can see from the foodchart below, you should eat:
  • A variety of different vegetables
  • Atleast 2 Fruit a day, however not to many as they are high in glucose i.e sugar
  • Grain foods, high in wholegrain, such as breads, cereals, rice, pasta, noodles, oats
  • Lean meats and poultry, fish, eggs, tofu, nuts and seeds
  • Milk, yoghurt and cheese
Foods you shouldn't each to much of, and mostly avoid, are:
  • High in saturated fat, such as biscuits, cakes and pastries.
  • Replace high fat foods which contain predominantly saturated fats such as butter and cream, with foods which contain predominantly polyunsaturated and monounsaturated fats such as oils.
  • Limit intake of foods and drinks containing added salt.
  • Do not add salt to foods at the table.
  • Limit intake of foods and drinks containing added sugars such as confectionary, sugar-sweetened soft drinks and cordials, fruit drinks, vitamin waters, energy and sports drinks.

You should always be mindful of the foods you are consuming. Continue to analyse the foodchart below, and then ask yourself the questions found in the left hand column. Make sure you are true and honest with yourself when answering the questions, because at the end of the day you will not be cheating anyone but yourself.

Ask yourself these questions

  • Do you skip breakfast, lunch or dinner more than once a week?
  • Do you eat less then 5 portions of vegetables per day?
  • Do you regularly eat high in sugar cereals, instead of healthier wholegrain alternatives?
  • Do you regularly choose white breads and rolls over their wholemeal alternatives?
  • Do you regularly drink sweet fizzy drinks instead of healthier alternatives like water?
  • Do you regularly eat cakes, sweets, chocolate or biscuits at school instead of healtheir alternatives?
  • Do you never check the food labels to see content in the products you're about to consume?

If you said YES to any of these questions, then you need to look at your diet and make a change. You can find some great healthy alternatives in the recipes section by just clicking here
