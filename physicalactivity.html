What is Physical Activity

Physical Activiy is any activity that gets you moving, makes your breathing become quicker, and your heart beating faster. Any movement that enhances your health can be included as a physical activity. The more active you are, the more you will benefit.

What are the benefits of physical activity

  • Burn more calories during physical activity, therefore maintaining a healthy weight. This therefore means there is less chance of being overweight or obese
  • Improved cardiovascualar fitness (heart and lungs)
  • Strengthens your bones and muscles
  • Improves your mental health and moods
  • Increases self-esteem
  • Increase your chances of living longer
  • Prevents or delays development of High Blood Pressure
  • Better sleep patterns
  • Improved posture
  • Increased flexibility
  • Reduce future risks of developing diabetes

What little things in daily life can you start doing to be active?

Physical activity doesn't need to be strenuous to be beneficial. Simple, non-strenuous activities can be counted as physical activity. Below are some examples from your everyday lives:

  • Dancing to your favourite tune
  • Riding bicycles or walking to places if its close by, instead of taking a lift or using public transport
  • Take the long way. E.g take the stairs instead of elevator
  • Swim at the local pool
  • Walking the dog
  • Throwing a frisbee at the local park
  • Rollerskating, rollerblading or skateboarding
  • Jumping on a trampoline
  • Help Mum or Dad with the gardening (You'll burn calories and get brownie points)
  • Flying a kite in the park or at the beach

So what should be the aim?

Try to work up to doing 150 minutes of moderate intensity aerobic activity a week. This can be something as simple as a 30 minute continuos walk or jog. If you're trying to lose weight however, try incorporating some high intensity interval training (HIIT) into your weekly schedule. This sort of training can be ideal for those whom don't have the time to do exercise for long periods. This involves doing an exercise/movement for a set amount of time at full throttle, and then taking a scheduled break in between. A great favourite of this website is jumping rope. You should always look to do your HIIT at a 2:1 ratio. A great starting jumprope workout is 1 minute of jumping rope, with 30 seconds rest inbetween. Do this for 10 repetions intially, and then keep increasing the repetitions. You'll be finished in 15 minutes. Recent studies have shown that this amount of HIIT training is the equivalent of a long aerobic jog of 30 minutes, so the benefits are their to be seen.
