What are Sedentary Behaviours

These are characterised by sitting or lying down (except for when sleeping obviously). These behaviours require very low energy expenditure, which means if you tend to do more of these then physical activities your body and health can suffer. Theres nothing neccessarly wrong with sedentary behaviours, but its important to limit the time you spend doing them. To much sedentary behaviour can lead to and include:

  • Lower levels of aerobic fitness
  • Higher risk of cardiovascular disease
  • Higher risk of type 2 diabetes
  • Higher risk of obesity or being overweight

How many of these Sedentary Behaviours do you do a day

  • Sitting whilst at school
  • Watching television
  • Using a computer or playing video games
  • Talking on the phone
  • Sitting while socialising with friends or family
  • Sitting in a car or other form of motorised transport

Alteranatives to these sedentary behaviours

  • Stand up and move during your favourite TV shows.
  • Get up to change the channel on the TV.
  • Stand or walk around while waiting for the bus or train.
  • Stand up while talking on the phone.
  • Set an alarm on your computer or electronic device to remind you to move more often.
  • Listen to recorded books as you do your cleaning or go for a walk, rather than sitting down to read.
  • Ride your bike to meet up with friends on the weekend.
  • Join a local group or team. Meet friends for a walk instead of a movie.
